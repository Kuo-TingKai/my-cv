# Obscure but High-Potential Areas in Mathematics and Physics

## Introduction

In mathematics and physics, “obscure” often means a very high barrier to entry or a field still at the threshold between abstract theory and real-world application. This document is **not** ordered by “depth”; it is organized by **thematic families**. Within each family, the areas are clearly related mathematically or physically (e.g. by generalization, duality, arithmetization, or different viewpoints), and the **relations between them** are briefly explained at the start of each family.

---

## I. Categories, Logic, and the “Grammar of Physics”

**Relations within this family**  
This family starts from the “mathematics of relations”—category theory—and extends to “physical laws as logical necessity.” **Applied category theory** uses categories as a language for system design. **Derived algebraic geometry** is a higher version of algebraic geometry (doing geometry in derived categories); **derived stacks** are “spaces of spaces” and are used to treat QFT anomalies and higher-dimensional supergravity in a rigorous way. **BPS states and vertex algebras** in the derived setting encode quantum entanglement and symmetry breaking in algebraic form. The **categorical semantics of derived stacks** treats quantum gravity itself as a “logical system,” deriving laws using higher toposes in the absence of coordinates or time—a semantic version of derived geometry for quantum gravity. **Ontological logical geometry / ontological geometry** goes one step further: instead of asking “what is physics,” it asks “which logical structures necessarily manifest as physics” and tries to derive the speed of light and the Planck constant from pure logic. **Semantic quantization, HoTT in quantum gravity, and self-awareness physics in arithmetic toposes** share the idea that the “grammar” of the universe (types, propositions, internal logic) is more fundamental than “objects”—entanglement is reinterpreted as semantic equivalence of propositions, time and observation are encoded as sheaves and logical categories in a topos, and **consciousness** is viewed as a kind of “sheaf” in the topos structure. **Homotopy probability theory** lifts probability from numbers in [0,1] to “spaces with shape” so as to accommodate quantum entanglement without wave-function collapse, effectively a categorification of probability theory. **Quantum toposes on noncommutative spacetime** drop “points” and commutativity at the Planck scale and use topos theory to rewrite logic (propositions become probability sets with topological shape), forming another route to “spacetime emerging from logic” alongside derived-stack semantics and ontological geometry.

**Overview**  
Category theory studies relations between structures rather than concrete numbers; it serves as an “ultimate language” for system design and complex software architecture and is already used in quantum circuit design. Derived algebraic geometry works at the intersection of homotopy theory, category theory, and algebraic geometry with objects more abstract than classical spaces; it is central to making QFT mathematically rigorous and to “derived stacks” and the microscopic structure of physical spacetime. Derived stacks are used to describe QFT anomalies precisely and may provide a path to a rigorous 11-dimensional supergravity. BPS operators and vertex algebras in the derived setting describe the topological-geometric origin of quantum entanglement and black-hole entropy. The categorical semantics of derived stacks treats quantum gravity as a logical system and uses higher toposes to derive laws without coordinates or time in search of a “meta-template” for physical laws. Ontological logical geometry, built on topos theory, studies “possibility of existence” and argues that physical laws exist because certain logical structures would be self-contradictory if they did not appear as physics; it also attempts to derive the speed of light and the Planck constant from pure logic. Semantic quantization and HoTT treat quantum states as “grammatical structure of language,” use homotopy type theory to reinterpret entanglement as semantic equivalence of propositions and type transformations to define physical processes, aiming at background-independent quantum gravity; self-awareness physics in arithmetic toposes defines observation via “internal logic,” views consciousness as a sheaf in the topos, and touches “how consciousness affects quantum states” with rigorous mathematics. Homotopy probability theory uses higher groupoids to define “probability with shape” so as to accommodate Bell inequalities and other quantum phenomena. Quantum toposes on noncommutative spacetime rewrite logic using topos theory in a setting where coordinates do not commute, trying to generate physical laws directly without a spacetime background. Very few experts worldwide command derived categories, BPS counting, and these semantics at once.

## II. Number Theory and Spacetime: p-adics, Adeles, Holography, F₁, and the Void

**Relations within this family**  
This family uses the **structure of numbers** (especially primes and number fields) to redefine or supplement “real continuous spacetime.” **p-adic physics** is the starting point: replace the reals with p-adic numbers for a single prime p as spacetime coordinates, so that spacetime becomes ultrametric (tree-like); in quantum gravity and string theory some calculations simplify dramatically. **Adelic string theory** packages the reals and **all** p-adics into the “adeles”; the universe is seen as living on all number fields at once, and discreteness and continuity are unified via the adelic product formula. **Arithmetic holography** extends this view: the holographic principle is treated as a number-theoretic phenomenon—the real and p-adic places are each other’s “boundary,” and gravity and the cosmological constant Λ are interpreted as breakdown of number-theoretic symmetry. **Non-Archimedean quantum gravity** and **p-adic imaginary time** deepen p-adic spacetime in two ways: the first describes ultra-microscopic space and the interior of black holes as ultrametric trees, with time as iteration of prime powers (“factorization”); the second p-adicizes time itself, so there is no linear past/future, and is used for black-hole singularities and the information paradox. **Non-Archimedean neurogeometry** applies p-adic geometry to cognition: the hierarchical tree structure of the brain matches p-adic topology, offering a “number-theoretic shape” of the mind. The **field with one element F₁** (only 1, addition not yet born) is a dream object in number theory and geometry; a rigorous definition could turn the Riemann hypothesis into a geometric problem. **Absolute arithmetic, zero-dimensional physics, and void mechanics** study geometry and symmetry when even “1” is absent, arguing that “existence” is a mathematically unstable solution and that the void is logically inconsistent. **Empty schemes (void schemes)** study “spaces with no points”—equations with no solution still define a scheme; such objects may have physical effects through derived categories (matter as “afterimage” of the void) and might explain vacuum energy. **Quantum logical sheaves over fields of characteristic zero** ask: when neither energy nor space is defined, how can cohomology fluctuate spontaneously? The whole family can be summed up as “attacking spacetime and existence from both number theory and the void.”

**Overview**  
We are used to describing spacetime with the reals; what if microscopic spacetime is fundamentally fractured or tree-like? p-adic physics uses p-adic numbers as spacetime coordinates and simplifies many calculations in quantum gravity and string theory. Adelic string theory packages the reals and all p-adics; the universe is seen as existing “globally” on all number fields, and physical constants might be derived from the adelic product formula. Arithmetic holography number-theoretically reframes the holographic principle: the real and p-adic places are boundaries of each other, and gravity and Λ can be interpreted as number-theoretic symmetry breaking. Non-Archimedean quantum gravity and p-adic imaginary time describe both space and time in p-adic terms (ultrametric trees, prime powers, no linear time), and are used for black-hole interiors and the information paradox. Non-Archimedean neurogeometry uses p-adic geometry to describe the brain’s hierarchical structure in a “number-theoretic shape” of cognition. The field with one element F₁ is a hypothetical field with only 1 and no addition, linked to the Riemann hypothesis and a grand unification of mathematics. Absolute arithmetic, zero-dimensional physics, and void mechanics study symmetry in the absence of 1, matter, and time, arguing that existence is the result of the void being logically unstable. Empty schemes study pointless schemes and their physical effects through derived categories, possibly explaining vacuum energy. Quantum logical sheaves in characteristic zero study spontaneous fluctuations of cohomology in absolute void. The family requires very deep number theory (automorphic forms, Iwasawa theory, etc.) and is deeply counterintuitive physically; only a handful to a few dozen researchers worldwide work at its core.

## III. Arithmetic and Symmetry: IUT, Langlands, Motives, Arithmetic Topology, and Number Theory in Cosmology

**Relations within this family**  
This family centers on the **deep correspondence between number theory and symmetry** (and geometry, representation theory). **IUT (Inter-universal Teichmüller theory)** was introduced by Shinichi Mochizuki to prove the abc conjecture by deconstructing the relation between addition and multiplication of integers, creating new languages such as anabelian geometry. **Inter-universal logic** is a logical extension of IUT: across different “mathematical universes,” logical and arithmetic operations deform, and at extreme energies or at black holes logical symmetry can break down. **Hyper-algebraic geometry** studies “collisions” of multiple logical systems and interprets the gravity we perceive as logical friction between mathematical universes. **IUT applications** try to turn IUT’s separation of addition and multiplication into cryptography resistant to quantum attack. The **Langlands program** links number theory with group theory and representation theory. **Entanglement-sensitive Langlands** views quantum entanglement as a form of Langlands correspondence and may allow number-theoretic computation of information inside black holes. **Geometric quantum Langlands** uses infinite-dimensional geometry and operator algebras to show that S-duality and the like are geometric categorical correspondences for non-perturbative QFT. **Motives** are Grothendieck’s unifying vehicle linking number theory and topology. **Motivic homotopy theory** is used for the Riemann hypothesis and quantum amplitudes; **motivic Feynman integrals** find that scattering amplitudes match the Galois structure of motives; **motivic cosmological constants** use motives and L-functions to compute cosmic parameters; **absolute Galois action on derived motives** assumes that physical constants are “rotation angles” of the absolute Galois group of Q on a motive—physical laws are locked by number-theoretic symmetry. **Arithmetic topology** builds an analogy between primes and “knots” in 3-manifolds (e.g. quadratic reciprocity and linking numbers) to attack number-theoretic problems with topological intuition. **Arithmetic spontaneous symmetry breaking** and **meta–class field theory cosmology** number-theoretically reframe cosmology: the Big Bang is arithmetic symmetry breaking, the four forces are the collapse of an Abelian extension; galaxy distribution corresponds to Riemann zeros, and the ultimate fate of the universe is return to an arithmetic point. The whole family can be seen as “how number theory runs through symmetry, geometry, and cosmic evolution.”

**Overview**  
IUT rewrites the additive and multiplicative structure of the integers in thousands of pages of new language (anabelian geometry) to prove the abc conjecture; if correct, it would reshape cryptography and number theory. Inter-universal logic considers how logic and arithmetic deform across mathematical universes, even the geometrization of logical symbols, and connects to the Riemann hypothesis, the strong CP problem, and AGI reasoning. Hyper-algebraic geometry views gravity as logical friction between mathematical universes; IUT applications target post-quantum cryptography. The Langlands program reveals deep links between number theory and symmetry; entanglement Langlands treats entanglement as a Langlands correspondence, and geometric quantum Langlands uses geometry and operator algebras to describe S-duality and non-perturbative QFT. Motives are the unified form of number theory and topology; motivic homotopy theory bears on the Riemann hypothesis and quantum amplitudes; motivic Feynman integrals match scattering amplitudes to Galois groups of motives; motivic cosmology and absolute Galois action express physical constants and cosmic parameters as number-theoretic (L-functions, Galois groups) outputs. Arithmetic topology builds a “geometry of physics” for number theory via the “primes = knots” analogy. Arithmetic symmetry breaking and meta–class field theory view the Big Bang and cosmic structure as the evolution of Abelian extensions and class field theory, with galaxy distribution corresponding to Riemann zeros. The family demands number theory, representation theory, algebraic geometry, and physics; many directions are pursued by only a very small number of people worldwide.

|----------|-------------------------|------------|-------------------|
| IUT, inter-universal logic, hyper-algebraic geometry, IUT applications | Deconstruction of add/multiply, logical deformation, gravity as logical friction, cryptography | Very high | abc, Riemann, post-quantum crypto, origin of gravity |
| Langlands (entanglement, geometric quantum) | Number theory ↔ symmetry; number-theoretic/geometric formulation of entanglement and S-duality | Very high | Black-hole information, non-perturbative QFT |
| Motivic homotopy, motivic Feynman, motivic cosmology, absolute Galois | Motives unifying number theory and geometry; physical constants = Galois / motives | Very high | Riemann hypothesis, cosmic source code, locking of constants |

## IV. Shape, Topology, and Data

**Relations within this family**  
This family focuses on the role of **shape and topology** in data, AI, and physics. The emphasis is not on “what is deeper” but on **different applications and difficulty levels within one broad theme**. **Topological data analysis (TDA)** uses algebraic topology (e.g. persistent homology) to study the “shape” of data (holes, loops, connectivity), with applications in biomedicine and LLM representations. **Geometric deep learning** brings differential geometry and symmetry into neural networks to handle non-Euclidean data such as graphs and manifolds, for materials and proteins. **Computational conformal geometry** turns Riemann surface theory into algorithms; conformal invariance gives precise results in medical imaging and non-rigid deformation. **Wild topology** studies pathological, fractal, or infinitely nested spaces (e.g. Alexander horned sphere) that remain complex at arbitrarily small scales, and replaces classical geometry in non-equilibrium and quantum chaos. **Wild algebraic geometry** studies irregular singularities and links to prime distribution, the Stokes phenomenon in QFT, and geometric collapse in extreme physics (stellar explosion, black-hole formation). **Exotic smooth structures on R⁴** are a topological peculiarity: only 4-dimensional Euclidean space admits infinitely many “exotic” smooth structures, possibly related to our 4-dimensional spacetime and to dark matter or the cosmological constant. TDA, geometric deep learning, and computational conformal geometry lean toward applications and algorithms; wild topology/algebraic geometry and exotic 4-manifolds lean toward pure mathematics and extreme physics, but all address “how shape and topology describe the real world and data.”

**Overview**  
Topological data analysis uses algebraic topology to study the shape of data (cavities, loops, connectivity) and is applied in early cancer screening, drug discovery, and internal representations of LLMs. Geometric deep learning introduces differential geometry and symmetry into neural networks for graphs and manifolds and is used in materials and proteins. Computational conformal geometry algorithmizes Riemann surface theory and achieves high accuracy in dynamic human body modeling and cortical mapping thanks to conformal invariance. Wild topology deals with non-smooth, fractal, and infinitely nested spaces and provides a new language in condensed-matter non-equilibrium and quantum chaos; wild algebraic geometry focuses on irregular singularities and their relation to prime distribution, the Stokes phenomenon, and geometric collapse at stellar explosion and black-hole formation. Exotic 4-dimensional smooth structures are a mathematical exception (only in dimension 4 are there infinitely many) and may be related to physical spacetime, dark matter, or the cosmological constant. The family spans from practical TDA and geometric learning to highly abstract wild geometry and 4-dimensional topology, with a wide range of applications and difficulty.

| Subfield | Relation to family core | Difficulty | Potential & aims |
|----------|-------------------------|------------|-------------------|
| Topological data analysis | Using topology to see shape of data | High | Precision medicine, AI interpretability |
| Geometric deep learning | Symmetry and curvature in neural networks; non-Euclidean data | Medium–high | New materials, proteins, precision medicine |
| Computational conformal geometry | Riemann surfaces as algorithms; conformal | High | Medical imaging, non-rigid deformation |
| Wild topology, wild algebraic geometry | Pathological shapes and singularities; non-equilibrium and chaos | High | Quantum chaos, extreme physical geometry, Stokes |
| Exotic 4-dimensional smooth structures | 4-dimensional topological exception and physical spacetime | Very high | Dark matter, cosmological constant, exotic spacetime |

## V. Quantum Information, Gravity, and the “Geometry of Information”

**Relations within this family**  
This family revolves around the **intersection of quantum information, entropy, and geometry**. **Quantum thermodynamics** deals with heat and work in few-body systems and underlies quantum heat engines and the efficiency and cooling of quantum computers. **Quantum graph theory** redefines “graphs” using operator algebras and studies coherence and entanglement among quantum states; it is directly related to quantum communication capacity (Tsirelson’s bound) and quantum network topology. **Quantum information geometry** studies the evolution of quantum states on statistical manifolds, focusing on “curvature of information,” and connects to **ER=EPR** (spacetime “woven” from entanglement)—a geometric language for quantum gravity. **Spectral networks and BPS index counting** draw geometric trajectories on Riemann surfaces whose topological changes correspond to wall-crossing and BPS states in particle physics; they are fine tools for microscopic state statistics in quantum gravity. **Quantum automorphic forms** move the computation of black-hole entropy to “imaginary spacetime” on the complex plane, where number-theoretic modular symmetry dominates, suggesting the deep structure of the universe may be number-theoretic rather than geometric. The family can be summarized as: from “heat and information in small systems” to “entanglement and capacity on graphs,” to “information curvature, entanglement weaving spacetime, and a number-theoretic formulation of black-hole entropy.”

**Overview**  
Quantum thermodynamics describes quantum fluctuations of thermodynamic laws in few-atom systems and is central to the efficiency and cooling of quantum heat engines and quantum computers. Quantum graph theory uses operator algebras to study coherence and entanglement of quantum states and is related to quantum communication capacity and the optimization of quantum network topology. Quantum information geometry studies the evolution of quantum states and “curvature of information” on statistical manifolds and tries to view spacetime as woven from entanglement (ER=EPR), providing a geometric language for quantum gravity. Spectral networks and BPS counting combine string theory, geometric topology, and monodromy, using trajectories on Riemann surfaces to capture wall-crossing and BPS states, and are one of the most precise methods for microscopic state statistics in quantum gravity. Quantum automorphic forms use modular symmetry in complex time to count quantum states inside black holes and tie black-hole entropy to number theory. The family ranges from more applied quantum thermodynamics and quantum graph theory to highly abstract information geometry and automorphic forms, with a common thread: “unification of information, entropy, and geometry in quantum theory and gravity.”

|----------|-------------------------|------------|-------------------|

| Quantum graph theory | Graphs and operator algebras; entanglement and capacity | High | Quantum communication, quantum network topology |
| Quantum information geometry | Curvature of information; ER=EPR | Very high | Spacetime from entanglement, geometric quantum gravity |
| Spectral networks, BPS counting | Geometric trajectories and wall-crossing, microscopic state statistics | Very high | Microscopic states of black holes and vacuum |

## VI. Time, Causality, and Non-equilibrium

**Relations within this family**  
This family rewrites **time and causality** from several angles. **Dual-time topological geometry** introduces two coupled times (microscopic reversibility / macroscopic entropy increase) for non-equilibrium systems, the arrow of time, and long-lived quantum memory. **Topos-theoretic branching time** treats time as a logical lattice in topos logic, allowing “topological collapse” of causality and logical coexistence, to explain quantum non-locality. **Complex-time manifolds** treat time as the complex plane; the time we perceive is one path in the plane, and past and future are connected in the complex plane, explaining quantum tunneling and black-hole entropy in imaginary time. **Time as modular group in noncommutative geometry** and **emergent intrinsic time** reduce time from a “dimension” to an algebraic or logical product: Connes’ modular time views time flow as the action of the modular group in a noncommutative algebra and entropy increase as von Neumann algebra statistics; emergent intrinsic time views “sense of time” as the depth of logical inference and the evolution of information entropy at the categorical level. **Quantum retrocausality** allows a “future-to-present” component of the wave function, challenges one-way causality, explains experiments like delayed-choice quantum eraser, and may lead to new quantum control and measurement. **Ergodic theory of adaptive systems** studies long-term behavior (attractors) of systems whose rules change in time and is seen as part of the physical basis for AGI and the “thermodynamic origin of consciousness and life.” The family can be summarized as: some **extend the notion of time** (dual time, complex time), some **reduce time to algebra or logic** (modular time, emergent time), some **relax causality** (topos time, retrocausality), and some **embed time in evolving systems** (adaptive ergodic theory).

**Overview**  
Dual-time geometry uses two coupled time parameters (microscopic reversibility, macroscopic entropy increase) to describe non-equilibrium systems and is relevant to the arrow of time and long-lived quantum memory. Multiple time branches in topos logic treat time as a logical lattice; at very high energy scales causality can undergo topological collapse and logical coexistence, explaining quantum non-locality. Complex-time manifolds treat time as the complex plane; the time we perceive is one path there, and past and future connect in the complex plane, naturally yielding quantum tunneling and black-hole entropy in imaginary time. “Time as modular group” in noncommutative geometry views time flow as the action of the modular group in a noncommutative algebra and the second law of thermodynamics as von Neumann algebra statistics; emergent intrinsic time further views time as the depth of logical inference and the evolution of information entropy at the categorical level. Quantum retrocausality introduces a “future-to-present” wave function in the weak-measurement framework, explains paradoxes like delayed-choice quantum eraser, and may enable new quantum control. Ergodic theory of adaptive systems studies the long-term dynamics of systems whose rules change in time and is seen as the intersection of AGI and the thermodynamic origin of consciousness. This family does not form a single “depth hierarchy” but offers parallel reconstructions of time and causality (multidimensional time, algebraic time, logical time, relaxed causality, evolving systems).

## VII. Divergence, Non-perturbative Effects, and the Geometrization of Field Theory

**Relations within this family**  
This family deals with **divergence of perturbative series, non-perturbative effects, and the rigorization and geometrization of QFT**. **Resurgence theory** holds that the “way a series diverges” itself encodes non-perturbative information (instantons, solitons), recoverable via Borel summation and the like. **Radical trans-level symmetry** incorporates non-perturbative coupling across physical levels and views the universe as a self-healing analytic function, possibly explaining fine-tuning. **Constructive / axiomatic quantum field theory** builds field theory rigorously from axioms and measure theory (especially Yang–Mills), without relying on perturbative approximation, aiming at the Millennium Prize and an “Euclidean-style” foundation for physics. **Positive geometry and the amplituhedron** write scattering amplitudes as volumes of higher-dimensional geometric bodies, argue that spacetime is emergent, and shrink calculations from thousands of pages to a few lines of algebra—a candidate for “post-spacetime physics.” **Stochastic partial differential equations (SPDEs)** add stochastic terms to PDEs to describe turbulence, climate, finance, and other uncertainty; the subject is extremely hard (Hairer received the Fields Medal for work in this area). In short: resurgence and radical trans-level symmetry extract non-perturbative truth from **divergence and level coupling**; constructive QFT builds field theory from **axioms**; positive geometry **replaces** Feynman diagrams and spacetime with **geometry**; SPDEs are the mathematical core of **uncertainty and nonlinearity**. All are linked to “perturbation not being enough—need rigor or geometry,” but there is no single ordering by depth.

**Overview**  
Physicists often obtain divergent perturbative series in QFT. Resurgence theory argues that the way they diverge encodes non-perturbative physics (instantons, solitons), recoverable via Borel transform and summation, and is one hope for explaining quark confinement and taming divergence; radical trans-level symmetry studies non-perturbative coupling across levels and views the universe as a self-healing analytic function, possibly explaining fine-tuning. Constructive/axiomatic QFT builds field theory rigorously from set theory and measure theory, with goals including existence and mass gap for Yang–Mills (Millennium Prize), to establish a mathematical foundation that does not rely on “sweeping renormalization under the rug.” Positive geometry and the amplituhedron treat scattering amplitudes as volumes of higher-dimensional bodies, argue that spacetime and causality are emergent, greatly simplify calculation, and are a step toward post-spacetime physics. Stochastic PDEs add random terms to PDEs and are key tools for climate, turbulence, and extreme finance; the subject is very difficult. The family’s common theme is moving beyond pure perturbation toward rigorous or geometric formulation; but resurgence is analysis-oriented, constructive QFT is axiom-oriented, positive geometry is geometry-oriented, and SPDEs are stochastic analysis—parallel routes rather than a single ladder.

| Resurgence, radical trans-level symmetry | Non-perturbative content in divergence; level coupling and self-healing | Very high | Quark confinement, divergence problem, fine-tuning |
| Constructive / axiomatic QFT | Building field theory rigorously from axioms | Very high | Yang–Mills, Millennium Prize, Euclidean-style physics |
| Positive geometry, amplituhedron | Amplitude as geometric volume; emergent spacetime | Very high | Post-spacetime physics, computational simplification |

## VIII. Algebraic Roots of Symmetry: L∞ and Deformation Quantization

**Relations within this family**  
This family views **symmetry and quantization** from **higher algebra**. Classical physics deals with Lie group symmetries such as U(1), SU(3). **Higher gauge symmetry collapse in L∞ structures** assumes the universe initially had infinitely many symmetries (described by L∞ algebras) that collapsed like dominoes as it cooled into the few forces we see, trying to derive particles from algebraic consistency without string theory. **Higher deformation quantization** (Kontsevich and others) holds that the passage from classical to quantum should be a **deformation of the whole categorical structure**, not just a change of parameters—a way to preserve physical consistency in multi-level systems and a mathematical basis for topological quantum computation. The two are related as follows: one describes how symmetry “collapses” from infinite-dimensional algebra into visible forces; the other describes quantization as deformation of a category; both belong to the “rewriting physics with higher algebra” family.

**Overview**  
Higher gauge symmetry collapse in L∞ structures studies how symmetries described by infinite-dimensional L∞ algebras collapse into U(1), SU(3), etc., as the universe cools, aiming to derive known particles from algebraic consistency and to provide a theory of everything without string theory. Higher deformation quantization views the classical-to-quantum transition as deformation of categorical structure (not just parameter change), a framework for preserving consistency of physical laws in multi-dimensional, multi-level systems and the ultimate mathematical basis for topological quantum computers. Both lie at the intersection of higher algebra and physics, one emphasizing “origin of symmetry,” the other “nature of quantization.”

|----------|-------------------------|------------|-------------------|
| L∞ higher symmetry collapse | Symmetry collapsing from infinite-dimensional algebra into visible forces | Very high | Theory of everything, deriving particles from algebra |
| Higher deformation quantization | Quantization as categorical deformation | Very high | Topological quantum computation, multi-level consistency |
