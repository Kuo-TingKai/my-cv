Introduction

In mathematics and physics, “obscure” often means a very high barrier to entry or a field still at the threshold between abstract theory and real-world application. This document is not ordered by “depth”; it is organized by thematic families. Within each family, the areas are clearly related mathematically or physically (e.g. by generalization, duality, arithmetization, or different viewpoints), and the relations between them are briefly explained at the start of each family.

I. Categories, Logic, and the “Grammar of Physics”

Relations within this family
This family starts from the “mathematics of relations”—category theory—and extends to “physical laws as logical necessity.” Applied category theory uses categories as a language for system design. Derived algebraic geometry is a higher version of algebraic geometry; derived stacks are “spaces of spaces” and are used to treat QFT anomalies and higher-dimensional supergravity rigorously. BPS states and vertex algebras in the derived setting encode quantum entanglement and symmetry breaking in algebraic form. The categorical semantics of derived stacks treats quantum gravity as a “logical system,” deriving laws using higher toposes without coordinates or time. Ontological logical geometry asks “which logical structures necessarily manifest as physics” and tries to derive the speed of light and the Planck constant from pure logic. Semantic quantization, HoTT in quantum gravity, and self-awareness physics in arithmetic toposes share the idea that the “grammar” of the universe (types, propositions, internal logic) is more fundamental than “objects.” Homotopy probability theory lifts probability to “spaces with shape” to accommodate quantum entanglement. Quantum toposes on noncommutative spacetime use topos theory to rewrite logic at the Planck scale.

Overview
Category theory studies relations between structures; it serves as a language for system design and is used in quantum circuit design. Derived algebraic geometry and derived stacks are central to QFT rigor and the microscopic structure of spacetime. BPS operators and vertex algebras describe the topological-geometric origin of entanglement and black-hole entropy. Categorical semantics of derived stacks treats quantum gravity as a logical system. Ontological logical geometry attempts to derive physical constants from pure logic. Semantic quantization and HoTT treat quantum states as grammatical structure; self-awareness physics in arithmetic toposes views consciousness as a sheaf in the topos. Homotopy probability theory uses higher groupoids for “probability with shape.” Quantum toposes on noncommutative spacetime rewrite logic where coordinates do not commute.

II. Number Theory and Spacetime: p-adics, Adeles, Holography, F₁, and the Void

Relations
This family uses the structure of numbers (primes, number fields) to redefine or supplement “real continuous spacetime.” p-adic physics replaces the reals with p-adic numbers as spacetime coordinates (ultrametric, tree-like). Adelic string theory packages the reals and all p-adics into the adeles. Arithmetic holography treats the holographic principle as a number-theoretic phenomenon. Non-Archimedean quantum gravity and p-adic imaginary time describe space and time in p-adic terms (black-hole interiors, information paradox). Non-Archimedean neurogeometry applies p-adic geometry to cognition. The field with one element F₁ is linked to the Riemann hypothesis. Absolute arithmetic, void mechanics, and empty schemes study geometry when “1” or points are absent; quantum logical sheaves study spontaneous cohomology fluctuations in the void.

Overview
p-adic physics simplifies calculations in quantum gravity and string theory. Adelic string theory unifies discreteness and continuity via the adelic product formula. Arithmetic holography reframes gravity and Λ as number-theoretic symmetry breaking. Non-Archimedean quantum gravity and p-adic imaginary time address black-hole interiors and the information paradox. F₁ could turn the Riemann hypothesis into a geometric problem. Void mechanics and empty schemes argue that existence is mathematically unstable; empty schemes may explain vacuum energy via derived categories.

III. Arithmetic and Symmetry: IUT, Langlands, Motives, Arithmetic Topology

Relations
This family centers on the correspondence between number theory and symmetry. IUT (Mochizuki) proves the abc conjecture by deconstructing addition and multiplication. Inter-universal logic and hyper-algebraic geometry extend this; IUT applications target post-quantum cryptography. The Langlands program links number theory with representation theory; entanglement-sensitive and geometric quantum Langlands connect to black-hole information and S-duality. Motives unify number theory and topology; motivic homotopy, motivic Feynman integrals, and motivic cosmology tie physical constants and cosmic parameters to Galois groups and L-functions. Arithmetic topology builds a “primes = knots” analogy. Arithmetic symmetry breaking and meta–class field theory cosmology view the Big Bang and cosmic structure as number-theoretic evolution.

Overview
IUT rewrites the integers in new language (anabelian geometry); inter-universal logic considers logic and arithmetic across mathematical universes. Langlands reveals deep links between number theory and symmetry; motivic methods express constants and amplitudes via motives and Galois groups. Arithmetic topology gives geometric intuition for primes; arithmetic cosmology reframes the universe’s evolution as class field theory.

IV. Shape, Topology, and Data

Relations
This family focuses on shape and topology in data, AI, and physics. Topological data analysis (TDA) uses persistent homology to study the shape of data. Geometric deep learning brings differential geometry and symmetry into neural networks for graphs and manifolds. Computational conformal geometry algorithmizes Riemann surface theory. Wild topology studies pathological, fractal spaces; wild algebraic geometry studies irregular singularities and links to prime distribution and Stokes phenomenon. Exotic smooth structures on R⁴ are a 4-dimensional topological peculiarity, possibly related to spacetime and dark matter.

Overview
TDA is applied in biomedicine and LLM representations. Geometric deep learning is used for materials and proteins. Computational conformal geometry achieves high accuracy in medical imaging. Wild topology and wild algebraic geometry apply to non-equilibrium and quantum chaos. Exotic 4-manifolds may relate to dark matter and the cosmological constant.

V. Quantum Information, Gravity, and the “Geometry of Information”

Relations
This family revolves around quantum information, entropy, and geometry. Quantum thermodynamics deals with heat and work in few-body systems. Quantum graph theory uses operator algebras for coherence and entanglement. Quantum information geometry studies “curvature of information” and connects to ER=EPR (spacetime from entanglement). Spectral networks and BPS counting draw geometric trajectories on Riemann surfaces for wall-crossing and microscopic state statistics. Quantum automorphic forms move black-hole entropy to “imaginary spacetime” with modular symmetry.

VI. Time, Causality, and Non-equilibrium

Relations
This family rewrites time and causality. Dual-time topological geometry uses two coupled times for non-equilibrium and the arrow of time. Topos-theoretic branching time treats time as a logical lattice. Complex-time manifolds treat time as the complex plane. Modular time and emergent intrinsic time reduce time to algebra or logic. Quantum retrocausality allows future-to-present components. Ergodic theory of adaptive systems studies systems whose rules change in time (AGI, thermodynamics of consciousness).

VII. Divergence, Non-perturbative Effects, and the Geometrization of Field Theory

Relations
This family deals with divergence of perturbative series, non-perturbative effects, and rigorization/geometrization of QFT. Resurgence theory encodes non-perturbative information in the way series diverge. Radical trans-level symmetry incorporates non-perturbative coupling across levels. Constructive/axiomatic QFT builds field theory from axioms (e.g. Yang–Mills). Positive geometry and the amplituhedron write amplitudes as volumes of geometric bodies; spacetime is emergent. Stochastic PDEs add randomness to PDEs for turbulence, climate, finance.

VIII. Algebraic Roots of Symmetry: L∞ and Deformation Quantization

Relations
This family views symmetry and quantization from higher algebra. Higher gauge symmetry collapse in L∞ structures assumes infinitely many symmetries (L∞ algebras) that collapsed into the few forces we see. Higher deformation quantization views the classical-to-quantum transition as deformation of the whole categorical structure—a basis for topological quantum computation.
